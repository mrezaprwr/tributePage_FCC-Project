Sir Aaron Rodgers

The Strongest of All

College Career

  • 2002 He threw 26 touchdown as a freshman at Butte Community College
  • 2003 Became the starting quarterback in the fifth game of 2003 season.
  • 2004 Rodgers set a school record for consecutive completed passes with 26 and tied an NCAA record with 23 consecutive passes completed in one game

Professional Career

  • 2005 NFL Draft Expected to be selected early and people commented him as a talented strong-armed junior
  • Backup years: 2005-2007 Agreed to a reported five-year $7.7 million deal and spent his rookie season with the 4-12 packer.
  • Transition to starter: 2008 Brett Favre retirement opened up the Packers starting quarterback position.
  • 2009 Rodgers recorded his first win in a comeback situation.
  • 2010 Road to superbowl XLV After winning the NFC Championship game, packers earned a trip to Super Bowl XLV.

Green Bay Packers quarterback Aaron Rodgers added another chapter of magic into his storybook career, only growing his legendary career.
